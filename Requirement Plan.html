1. Introduction

1.1 Purpose

The Requirements Management Plan outlines the approach, processes, and tools that will be used to manage the requirements of the NexGen Video Surveillance Management Studio project. This plan ensures that all requirements are captured, analyzed, documented, verified, and managed throughout the project lifecycle to ensure alignment with business goals and user needs.

1.2 Objectives

  • Ensure all stakeholder requirements are accurately captured and documented.
  • Maintain a clear and organized repository of requirements.
  • Facilitate effective communication among stakeholders regarding requirements.
  • Track and manage changes to requirements.
  • Ensure traceability of requirements from inception through implementation and testing.

2. Roles and Responsibilities

Role Responsibilities
Product Owner (Jennifer Alexander) Define vision and goals, prioritize features, approve requirement changes
Business Analyst Gather and document requirements, conduct requirements analysis, facilitate requirements validation sessions
Project Manager Ensure adherence to the requirements management plan, coordinate requirements-related activities, manage scope changes
Development Team Provide technical input on requirements feasibility, implement requirements
Quality Assurance (QA) Team Validate requirements through testing, ensure requirements are testable and measurable

3. Requirements Identification and Documentation

3.1 Requirement Elicitation

  • Conduct stakeholder interviews, surveys, and workshops.
  • Analyze existing systems and documentation.
  • Use techniques such as user stories, use cases, and scenarios.

3.2 Requirement Documentation

  • Document requirements in a clear, concise, and structured format.
  • Use a requirements management tool (e.g., JIRA, Confluence) to store and organize requirements.
  • Ensure each requirement has a unique identifier for traceability.

4. Requirements Analysis

  • Categorize requirements into functional and non-functional.
  • Prioritize requirements based on business value, urgency, and dependencies.
  • Conduct feasibility analysis and impact assessment.
  • Review and validate requirements with stakeholders.

5. Requirements Traceability

  • Establish traceability matrices to link requirements to business objectives, design documents, test cases, and implementation tasks.
  • Ensure each requirement is traceable through all stages of the project lifecycle.

6. Requirements Change Management

6.1 Change Control Process

  • Submit change requests using a standardized form.
  • Assess the impact of changes on scope, schedule, and budget.
  • Review and approve/reject change requests in a Change Control Board (CCB) meeting.

6.2 Version Control

  • Use version control to manage changes to requirements documents.
  • Maintain a change log to record all changes, approvals, and rationales.

7. Requirements Validation and Verification

  • Conduct regular reviews and walkthroughs to validate requirements.
  • Ensure requirements are testable and measurable.
  • Develop test cases and acceptance criteria based on requirements.
  • Perform user acceptance testing (UAT) to verify requirements are met.

8. Tools and Techniques

  • Use requirements management tools (e.g., JIRA, Confluence) for documentation, tracking, and traceability.
  • Utilize modeling tools (e.g., UML, BPMN) for visual representation of requirements.
  • Apply techniques such as user stories, use cases, scenarios, and mockups.

9. Communication and Reporting

  • Conduct regular meetings to review and discuss requirements.
  • Provide status updates on requirements progress and changes.
  • Distribute requirement documents and updates to all relevant stakeholders.

10. Review and Approval

  • Requirements must be reviewed and approved by key stakeholders, including the Product Owner and Project Manager.
  • Ensure all stakeholders sign off on the final requirements before proceeding to the design and implementation phases.

11. Training

  • Provide training to team members on requirements management processes and tools.
  • Conduct workshops to ensure stakeholders understand their roles and responsibilities in the requirements process.

12. Metrics and Monitoring

  • Track metrics such as the number of requirements gathered, changes requested, and defects found during testing.
  • Monitor requirements status and progress through regular reports and dashboards.

13. Conclusion

The Requirements Management Plan ensures that all project requirements are effectively captured, analyzed, documented, and managed throughout the project lifecycle. Adhering to this plan will help ensure the success of the NexGen Video Surveillance Management Studio by meeting stakeholder needs and delivering a high-quality product.
