1. Introduction

1.1 Purpose

The purpose of this document is to outline the vision for the NexGen Video Surveillance Management Studio project, detailing its objectives, stakeholders, scope, and key requirements. This document will serve as a guiding framework for the development, deployment, and operation of the project, ensuring alignment with business goals and user needs.

1.3 Scope

The NexGen Video Surveillance Management Studio project aims to create an advanced system for managing video surveillance, including real-time monitoring, AI-based analysis, multi-camera support, and robust data storage solutions. The project will include the development of a web-based interface, backend services, and AI models to enhance security and operational efficiency.

2. Business Case

2.1 Business Opportunity/Need

The NexGen Video Surveillance Management Studio addresses the need for efficient, scalable, and intelligent video surveillance solutions. Organizations are increasingly seeking advanced systems that can provide real-time monitoring, AI-driven analytics, and support for large-scale environments. This project aims to meet these demands by offering a comprehensive solution that enhances security and operational efficiency.

2.2 Problem Statement

Current video surveillance systems often lack the capability to handle large-scale environments and provide real-time AI-based analysis. These limitations lead to delayed responses to security incidents, increased workload on human operators, and higher operational costs. The NexGen Video Surveillance Management Studio aims to overcome these challenges by offering a robust and scalable solution with advanced features.

4.2 Benefits

  • Efficiency: Automates video analysis and incident detection, reducing the workload on human operators.
  • Scalability: Can handle large-scale environments with multiple cameras and high data volumes.
  • User Satisfaction: Provides a comprehensive and user-friendly interface for managing and analyzing video feeds.
  • Cost Savings: Reduces the need for extensive manual monitoring, lowering operational costs.

6. Dependencies and Assumptions

Dependencies

  • Reliable internet connection for cloud-based AI processing.
  • Availability of training datasets for AI model development.

Assumptions

  • Users have access to devices with internet connectivity.
  • The organization has the necessary infrastructure to support the surveillance management studio.
  • Stakeholders will provide timely feedback during development.
  • There are no unforeseen regulatory changes affecting the project.
  • Third-party services used for translation and other functionalities will remain operational.

7. Cost and Pricing

  • Manpower: Estimated cost for hiring developers, data scientists, and other key roles.
  • Software: Licensing fees for AI tools, video processing software, and other applications.
  • Hardware: Costs for servers and other infrastructure required for deployment.
  • Third-Party Services: Fees for translation services and other external APIs.

8. Quality Control

  • Quality Assurance: Regular testing cycles including unit tests, integration tests, and user acceptance testing.
  • Quality Control: Continuous monitoring and logging to identify and resolve issues promptly.
  • Tools: Use of automated testing tools and CI/CD pipelines to ensure consistent quality.
